Why are you here?

Do you have pain, tingling or the feeling that your body is compensating because of a muscle imbalance?

Have you tried rest, medication, massage or exercises, but the symptoms keep coming back?

That usually means the painful area may be only one part of the problem. The next step is to check how you move, load your body and control the muscles around the problem.

Physiotherapy in Sofia: how we work at DM Physio

We do not ask you to choose a procedure blindly. We begin with a functional assessment of posture, movement, strength, muscle control and daily habits, so the therapy plan is based on what your body actually shows.

Activating underused muscles

Pain is often influenced not only by what is overloaded, but also by what is not contributing enough. We check which muscles need better activation and control, then build them back into movement.

Releasing overloaded areas

When muscles stay tense or shortened, they can change how joints move and increase load in nearby areas. We identify these patterns and use manual techniques, movement and exercises to restore better balance.

Understanding the mechanism

Progress is easier when you understand why symptoms return. We explain which positions, habits or loading patterns may keep the problem active and how to adjust them.

At DM Physio, we do not focus only on the symptom - we look for the factors that provoke and maintain it.

What the recovery process looks like

Everyday stress on the body - long sitting, repetitive movement, posture, training load or work positions - can gradually create muscle imbalance, stiffness and recurring tension. The goal is to understand these factors and build a plan that fits your body.

Common conditions

Temple pain

Headache and temple tension can be related to muscle overload, posture and sensitivity around the head and neck.

Cervical disc herniation

A cervical disc problem may be linked with neck pain, arm pain, tingling or changes in how the arm feels.

Piriformis syndrome

Piriformis-related irritation can refer pain or tingling through the buttock and down the leg.

Lower back pain

Lower back pain can involve several factors: posture, load, muscle control, mobility and daily habits.
