Relaxing massage - step by step

Relaxing Massage

step by step

Relaxation massage is a gentle and soothing therapy that aims to reduce stress, relax tense muscles and improve overall well-being. Unlike remedial or deep tissue massage, the focus is on creating a sense of calm and harmony through smooth and rhythmic movements.

Procedure (90 min.)

During the relaxation massage, techniques such as stroking, rubbing, kneading and tapping are used, targeting the surface layers of the muscles. The goal is to stimulate the parasympathetic nervous system for deep relaxation and stress reduction. Benefits include:

  • Reducing stress and anxiety - stimulates the production of endorphins.
  • Improving sleep — deeper and more restful sleep.
  • Improvement of blood circulation — better supply of tissues with oxygen.
  • Indirect immune support — by lowering stress hormones.

Relaxing massage is a great way to take care of yourself, recharge your energy and improve your overall mental and physical health.

Relaxation Massage Frequently Asked Questions

Who is a relaxing massage suitable for?

It is suitable for almost anyone looking for relief from stress, tension and fatigue. Ideal for people with long periods of sitting, light cramps or "me time".

How often can I have a relaxing massage?

As needed and budgeted — often once a week or every two weeks, as part of a stress management routine.

Are there any side effects?

It is usually safe and rarely has side effects. You may feel a little sleepy or lethargic after the massage. Drink water after the session.

What should I expect during a relaxing massage?

A quiet, peaceful setting and gentle, smooth movements. You will be covered with a towel; only the areas being worked on are detected, with comfort and relaxation as a priority.
